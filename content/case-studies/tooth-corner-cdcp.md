## The brief

**Tooth Corner** — Ontario dental DSO with 30+ locations across the province [client brief, 2026]. Active engagement (2026). Objective: own the long tail of CDCP-driven patient acquisition queries across Canada, with primary capture in Ontario.

## What CDCP did to dental search

The Canadian Dental Care Plan (CDCP) — federal coverage launched in phases through 2024 [Government of Canada][^1] — created a new class of search intent overnight. Phrases like "dentists accepting CDCP near me" and "CDCP-covered dental services [city]" emerged as a wave of new queries with high commercial intent and almost no entrenched ranking competitors.

## The mapping

Autosuggest scrape across the CDCP query universe × 152 Canadian cities produced approximately **2,000+ unique long-tail combinations** [autocomplete scrape dataset, 2026]. Each maps to one of three intent classes:

1. **Eligibility / informational** — owned by Government of Canada by default [Google SERP data, 2026][^1]
2. **Provider-finder** — "dentists accepting CDCP [city]" — primary commercial intent
3. **Service-scoped** — "CDCP-covered cleanings [city]" — high-conversion long tail

## The architecture

Standard corporate-multisite-reconciliation methodology [Sterling Sky / Joy Hawkins][^2]:

- **One corporate site** carrying schema-rich location pages for every clinic
- **One GBP per physical location** with "Department of" relationships declared upward
- **City-scoped neighbourhood pages** below each location
- **CDCP-specific landing pages** at the corporate level, syndicated per location

## The audit framework that scales

A 40-factor Map Pack audit framework [Whitespark Local Search Ranking Factors, 2025][^3] — augmented by a healthcare-DSO overlay — deployable across 130-client cohorts in batch.

Output per location: factor scorecard, competitor leaderboard, category visibility score, recovery roadmap, and dollar-quantified opportunity sizing.

## Where this is going

Active engagement, in flight. Mapping done. Architectural recommendations written. Deployment phase next — 30+ locations.
