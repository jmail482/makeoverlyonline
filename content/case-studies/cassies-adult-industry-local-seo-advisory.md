## The situation

Daniel operated **Cassie's (cassies.com.au)**, an adult entertainment business across five Australian locations: New South Wales (Sydney), Victoria, and three additional states [client brief, Sep 2022]. He was ranking adequately for primary metropolitan targets but sought stronger visibility in smaller city and suburban markets.

**Engagement: September 26, 2022.**

## The constraints

The adult industry classification created documented limitations [engagement records, Sep 2022]:

- **Citation directories:** many declined to work with adult content businesses
- **Backlink vendors:** same restriction applied
- **GMB verification:** required exterior business signage photography — difficult for a business without a conventional retail storefront [^1]
- **Competitors:** including search keywords directly in their GMB business name field in violation of Google's guidelines[^2] — Google was not acting on reports despite them being filed

## What the initial audit found

The GMB profile was not optimised — the description was too short and lacked keyword coverage [GBP audit, Sep 2022]. Facebook and LinkedIn business pages were both absent [social audit, Sep 2022]. BrightLocal citation management was already underway for Sydney [BrightLocal account, Sep 2022][^3].

## What the audit recommended

- **GMB description:** Expand and optimise within category limitations
- **Facebook and LinkedIn business pages:** Establish both
- **BrightLocal:** Continue citation completion for Sydney [^3]
- **Competitor name-stuffing:** Do not replicate — long-term enforcement risk outweighs short-term advantage [^2]
- **Secondary markets without a physical GBP listing:** On-page location targeting and blog posts

## What this demonstrates

Constraint mapping first — what's available, what isn't, and what can realistically be done within those limits — is the correct approach when standard playbook tools are unavailable.
